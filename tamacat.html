A tangible pet that you can interact with both physically as well as in AR

Project

For the lecture Mixed Reality and Tangible Interaction at the Université Paris-Saclay, me and a group of two others designed and implemented a tangible cat, that can drive around, and that can be interacted with using AR on the smartphone, inspired by a Tamagotchi.
For this we used Arduino, 3D printing, and Unity to implement this. The user can interact on their smartphone by feeding the cat, giving it a scratch tree, in the form of a palm tree, and lay it to sleep in its cat bed. Also with the use of a touch sensor, it can be petted. It is shown in the video on the bottom of the page.

My Role & Key Learnings

In the team I acted as designer and co-implementor. In the beginning I lead the ideation session to find a solution we would like to implement. In the implementation, I designed the AR tags that enable the AR interaction, added the animations in Unity, and assisted in the development of the Arduino cat, due to my previous knowledge in hard-ware prototyping.
In this project I learned a lot about AR solutions and how to implement them. They can be a fun and interactive solution to tangible solutions or use cases in the physical world. I also got to continue developing my knowledge in hard-ware prototyping.
