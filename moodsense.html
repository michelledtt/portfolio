A machine learning based mood tracker that facilitates keeping the emotions in track and communicating with your therapist

Project

For the course Interactive Machine Learning at Université Paris-Saclay, me and a group of two others collaborated on designing a machine learning based mood tracker, which facilitates keeping a diary of the mood, which can be used as therapy tool.
Mood trackers can normally be very repetitive to use, which leads to users forgetting to do it and then abandoning it. But by creating an app that makes it easier as it can recognize the mood from a selfie, it makes the process more attractive and easier to keep doing it.
This approach allows the collaboration between the ML experts (who train and test the emotion recognition models), the therapists (who need to understand and enhance the ML predictions), and finally the end-users the patients, who track their emotional states and are able to provide detailed qualitative descriptions about them. We used a existing database called AffectNet and personalized it by adding personal training data, which helped to recognize the moods happy, angry and sad.

My Role & Key Learnings

In the team we all had a background in Human-Computer Interaction so our skills overlapped. This made it an opportunity to collaborate on the designs and also the implementation, which always help in iterating the product and making it better. My role, in more detail, was to design the patient app, and implement it. I designed the wireframe as a sketch and then implemented it due to time pressure in HTML but in an app optic. I also used Marcelle, a toolkit to implement machine learning based solutions, to achieve the machine learning background.
This project helped me to get a deeper background in AI-based solutions and how to implement them (in a smaller scale). It also gave me practice in designing an app.
