A serious game that supports stroke patients in their rehabilitation

Project

For the course Serious Games at Université Paris-Saclay, me and a group with two others designed a serious game to make stroke rehabilitation more motivating and adaptive, inspired by Guitar Hero.
The approach combines the accessibility of at-home rehabilitation with the engaging elements of a music-driven and cooperative game. The configurability for doctors and the emphasis on adaptability aim to create a motivating and effective rehabilitation experience. By integrating these elements, the application aims to provide a motivating, customizable, and accessible platform for hand rehabilitation, addressing the challenges and needs identified in the literature.

My Role & Key Learnings

In the team I acted as researcher and designer as the other two team members had more experience in technical implementation. I pushed the project forward during the literature review, and created the prototype of the game itself.
In this project I learned approaches to design a serious game, which has the strength of making unpleasant but important activities more motivating. I also continued to develop my leader skills, as this was important to progress the project.
